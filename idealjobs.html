Ruth's Ideal Job.Ethical Hacker

After deciding upon a career in IT I also investigated what jobs there are. I immediately was interested in becoming an Ethical Hacker. An ethical Hackers job is to ensure a business or organisations computer systems are safe. As in the title of the job they are basically hired to hack into the company or organisations network as to identify where any weak areas or issues may lie. This role is known as penetration tester within Australia.

My father owns his own accountancy business. One day he was hacked, and the hacker took months and months' worth of data he had been working on for various clients and demanded money in exchange. My father decided not to give in to these demands and as a result lost a lot of work.

When I read about the Ethical Hacker role, I immediately remembered what had happened and I would love the opportunity to help business/ organisations to be safe from these cyber criminals.

In order to become an Ethical Hacker you firstly would need a bachelor's degree in IT, cybersecurity or engineering etc. Due to the level of responsibility of this role you would need to gain some relevant experience either in entry level IT roles or network admin etc. After a period of sometime most organisations would offer various programs where you would be able to gain certifications for ethical hacking fields. Once these steps have been completed you would then be able to be considered for a role. Normally companies looking for ethical hackers require 4+ years of experience once again due to the responsibility of the role.

As previously mentioned, my highest qualification is a Diploma in accountancy, nor do I possess any experience in computing apart from school level. This would not meet the criteria for this role. My Plan is to obtain a bachelor's degree in IT once I have completed this course I plan on obtaining an entry level role which would provide a career path to further my knowledge and enable me to gain whether it be voluntary or paid in programs that lead to certifications in ethical hacking, security analysis, pen testing and related fields.

Matt's Ideal Job. Systems Engineer/Project Engineer

https://www.seek.com.au/job/50544467?type=standout#searchRequestToken=3ca1c47b-2525-4ad5-94bb-fb24ef1fefd7

The ideal role I will be aiming for is the Systems Engineer/Project Engineer. The position allows you to have the most significant input into developing, building, and maintaining a software environment for any business, whether it is a new start-up or an existing multinational corporation. You are given a problem from a client, and you have to come up with a solution that fits the best.

This role's appeal is that you can be at the ground level of a whole new development or come into an existing environment and put your personal touches on it.

Required skills, qualifications, and experience

The skills that you need for this role range from technical, logical, and personal.

You have to understand the software and the environment and how it operates. The technical skills could become very niche, also with new technologies emerging all the time you have to keep your skill set up to date.

You have to have a systems thinking and critical thinking background which comes into the logical skill. The client will have a complex problem, and you will need to provide them with a simple solution and explain it to them.

This is where personal skills will come into play. You will need to be able to take all that technical information and translate it so that a non-technical minded person will understand what you are saying.

Qualifications and certificates on Windows Server, Linux, specific software, will all be needed; having a Bachelors on Information Technology would put you in a more favourable position, having Masters of Systems Engineering would be even better.

The experience will range from project to project, but having at least 5+ years of doing similar administration type roles would be required for my expectations.

Current skills, qualifications, and experience

My current skill set is very close to what I have described above; I have an excellent baseline in understanding how small to large environments work and what kind of software should be utilised for the client base.

The biggest skill I am currently lacking would be the systems and critical thinking area. I have only just begun my research and education into these skills. I can further my education with a Masters of Systems Engineering, which would cover my current lack of skill in this field.

I believe I have very strong personal skills as I have worked in many customer facing industries which have given me the ability to talk to customers and get points across that may be technical in design. I can turn the technical jargon into something that can be processed by someone who isn't as technical minded.

My current qualifications range from Windows Server, MS Exchange, SharePoint, Azure Administration, SCCM/SCOM, CISCO, and RedHat/CentOS Linux. Plus, I have a few extra courses such as SPLUNK, ITIL and COMPTIA Security+.

I've spent the last five years in a Systems Administration role where I have been exposed to a few different software environments where I have performed level 1 to level 3 support. Supporting client bases of a single person up to 500 users.

Plan of action

My current plan is to get into the Systems Engineer/Project Engineer role will involve learning more about the systems and critical thinking aspect of being a Systems Engineer.

With my current role, I have access to existing System Engineers, and I will try to work under them from time to time better to understand the day-to-day work of a Systems Engineer.

I will continue to keep my skills up to date and research new technologies as they emerge.

I will also work towards my Bachelors of Information Technology.
